Accusative is the done-to of a verb: man bites dog

Perfect aspect

Perfect aspect in English is exemplified in ‘I have read this book’,

which expresses an event that took place before the time spoken

but which has an effect on or is in some way still relevant to the present.

In Martian, the perfect is constructed with an affix

Derivational morphology

  • Adjective → noun
    • Suffix /-eɲ/
  • Noun → adjective
    • Suffix /-(æ)ʃ/
  • Noun → verb
    • Suffix /-(ɔ)xə/
  • Verb → noun
    • Suffix /-(i)ɲ/
  • Verb → adjective
    • Suffix /-(u)ʃ/
  • Adjective → verb
    • Suffix /-i/
  • Adjective → adverb
    • Suffix /-æ/
  • One who Xs (e.g. paint → painter)
    • Suffix /-(ɔ)xə/
  • Place of (e.g. wine → winery)
    • Suffix /-(æ)ʤ/
  • Diminutive
    • Suffix /-(æ)ʤ/
  • Augmentative
